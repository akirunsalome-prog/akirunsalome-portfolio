Key Insights & Optimisations

Google Ads Growth Strategy

The Challenge

A UK student nightlife ticketing platform that had previously run Google Ads with poor results — high costs, no measurable ROAS. Following a rebrand, organic traffic had also declined significantly. The core constraint: average ticket prices of £5–£7. At that margin, a CPA above £4 makes the channel entirely unviable before accounting for any other business costs. Every structural decision in this strategy flows from that single constraint.

Strategy & Campaign Architecture

Three Search-only campaigns structured by intent level. No Display, no Performance Max, no video — Search only, because it captures people already in-market, which is the most efficient use of a budget this constrained at this ticket price.

Campaign 1 — Brand Recovery · £5/dayCaptures warm traffic still searching under the previous brand name. Quickest win in the account. Low CPC, high intent, minimal competition.
Campaign 2 — High Intent Student Nights · £8/dayCore acquisition campaign. Active in-market buyers searching for a night out now. Every ad routes to the specific event page, not the homepage.
Campaign 3 — Venue-Specific Search · £3.50/dayUsers searching by venue name are close to a purchase decision. Campaign routes directly to the event page, removing friction between search and purchase.
Negative Keyword StrategyAccount-level negatives from Day 1: "free," "jobs," "refund," "reviews," "wikipedia." For a business this CPA-sensitive, one irrelevant click is a missed conversion.

Bidding Progression

PhaseBiddingObjective
Days 1–30Manual CPCGather data. Identify winning search terms. Control spend tightly.
Days 31–60Maximise ConversionsLet Google begin optimising toward conversions using early data signals.
Day 60+Target CPAMove to Target CPA once sufficient conversion volume supports the algorithm.

Strategic Rationale

The strategic goal of Month 1 is not just ticket sales — it's proving the channel works at this margin. Once 75–100 conversions are logged, the account gains sufficient data for Smart Bidding to take over and push CPA progressively lower without increasing spend. The architecture is built to scale, not just to launch.
